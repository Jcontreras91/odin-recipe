Braised Beef

Description

A versatile weekend dinner recipe that has delicious left overs for days

Picture this, a Sunday evening braised beef dinner from years past. You can smell the delicious aroma that filled the air. You can taste those magnificent flavor and, you can feel the happiness that once filled the air.

This memory is exactly what this recipe will bring. Although it is a long cook, You will rejoice not only in its flavor, but in the memories that it brings.

Ingredients

  • 3-4 lbs of beef chuck(sliced evenly into 4x6 inch pieces)
  • kosher salt
  • pepper
  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • 1 large carrot, chopped
  • 1 yellow onion, chopped
  • 1 rib of celery, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon of tomato paste
  • 2 cups of chicken broth
  • 1 cup of red wine
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 sprig of thyme
  • 2 tablespoons of flour (optional)

  1. Preheat oven to 300°F (150°C). Generously coat beef with salt, followed by a light coat of freshly ground pepper. If you are using flour, also add that now In a large pot, bring oil up to a simmer. 1 by 1 place the beef into the pot. Allow to cook until a thick crust forms on each side.

  2. Place browned meat on a platter. Bring the oil in the pot back up to a simmer Add the carrots, celery, and onions. Stir frequently, and cook until softend. Add tomato paste and cook until for 30 seconds.

  3. Add the red wine and scrape up the burnt bits on the bottom of the pot. After 1 minute, to the pot, add the chicken stock, beef, bay leaf, and thyme.

  4. cook for 2-3 hours until meat is fork tender. After time has past, place meat onto platter and serve.
